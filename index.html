> About

I am a candidate for B.S. in Mathematics, Computer Science at Carnegie Mellon University. I'm interested in various fields of computer science, including software engineering, algorithmic trading, and machine learning research. This summer, I'm working as a software development engineering intern at Amazon, where I have the opportunity to design and develop a native AWS service. Using AWS Cloud Development Kit and Software Development Kit, I'm working on building a vertically scalable event-driven service for handling fast write requests ( ≤ 5ms latency) into a 20 Terabyte datastore.


On campus, I am working as an AI/ML Research Assistant at the Mohimani Lab, where I co-authored a research paper that developed a transformer-based Protein Language Model for predicting the structure of NRPSs from their amino acid sentences. NRPSs are large modular enzymes that produce a wide variety of natural products, including antibiotics, antifungals, and even anticancer drugs.


I'm also a TA for 15-122: Principles of Imperative Computation In this role, I help teach and answer questions relating to programming in C, data structures such as AVL trees/heaps and algorithms such as Huffman Coding. Functions are Pointers!
