Using Deep Neural Networks
for the Early Diagnosis of
Parkinson's Disease

This past year, I decided to work on developing a predictive diagnosis method for the early detection of Parkinson's Disease (PD). In brief, PD is a neurolgical disorder that hinders a person's movement and cognitive abilities. Traditionally, the method of diagnosis has been a clinical examination, which is not very good at predicting future prognosis. In order to solve this problem, I decided to develop a neural network that analyzes a patient's brain MRI scans and determines if they are showing any early signs for PD. Using this newly developed tool, I then tested two different diagnositc protocols and determine which one produced the highest accuracies. Refer to my published work here for more details.



A Comprehensive Application
for the Early Detection of
Dementia

While working on the cognitive symptoms of my research project, I noticed a lack of comprehensive tools that elderly people can use to keep track of their mental state of being. So, as an addition to my research project, I also decided to work on creating a cohesive web application that users can use intermittently to make sure they are not showing early signs of dementia.

The general data flow for the app is as folows: users upload speech recordings of themselves answering certain questions taken from the Mini Mental Status Examination (MMSE) to the website. These files are then sent to a private server hosted by Flask, where they are analyzed for speech biomarkers. At the end, the custom written algorithm will produce a score that represents the user's mental well being. This score is sent back to the website so that the user can see how they performed. The user is asked to take the test again over the next few months. If there is a general downward trend to the user's scores, they are alerted to visit the doctor's office and get tested for neurological diseases. Refer to my Github repo for more information
